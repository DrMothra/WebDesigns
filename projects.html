Projects

I've been fortunate enough to work on a diverse range of research projects. Some of the them are outlined below.

  • MAGELLAN

    • MAGELLAN (a Multimodal Authoring and Gaming Environment for Location-based coLlaborative AdveNtures) is concerned with developing the next generation of authoring tools for creating and distributing location-based experiences. The tool will cater for a diverse range of user experience in allowing participants to create engaging applications.

  • CHESS

    • CHESS (Cultural Heritage Experiences through Socio-personal Interactions and Storytelling) aimed to enhance interactive experiences at visitor attractions such as museums and galleries, etc. by deploying a range of technologies to augment the interactions.

  • Glu4D

    • The Glu4D system is an example of mixed reality architecture whereby elements of the virtual (a virtual environment) are combined with elements of the real physical world, in this case real-time video feeds from other participants. The system allows multiple participants to communicate and share resources whilst potentially being located by considerable distances.

  • Cospatial

    • Cospatial (Communication and Social Participation : Collaborative Technologies for Interaction and Learning) utilised collaborative virtual environments to attempt to improve the social skills of young adults on the Autistic Spectrum Disorder.

  • Flypad

    • Flypad was a multiplayer collaborative mixed reality game situated in a public gallery. Visitors to the attractions were able to participate in the physics-based game where physical exertions were rewarded via swapping virtual limbs with other players!

  • Simulation

    • Virtual surgery allows practitioners to simulate complex procedures prior to performing the operations for real. The simulator allowed real-time deformation and cutting of brain tissue all with appropriate haptic feedback.

  • Medical Imaging

    • As a consequence of developing the surgery simulator outlined above there was a need to design software tools to enable the anatomical structures of the brain to be visualised and modelled. Three-dimensional models of the brain were subsequently produced by the software for use in the simulator to enable patient-specific operations to be performed.
