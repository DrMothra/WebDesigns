Research

Technologies currently occupying my time

Research

I have been actively involved in many research topics over the years and the main themes have centred around three-dimensional computer graphics and interface design. Projects have included virtual reality, collaborative virtual environments, education, simulation, visualisation and the creation of a whole host of tools to support the work.

Currently I am developing a number of web-based applications. These consist of a "trajectory" sketching tool (TrajectASketch) to aid in the creation and visualisation of the concepts of Trajectories and exploring 3D visualisations on the web via WebGL.

WebGL

One of the particularly interesting developments in 3D graphics at the moment is the emergence of WebGL, providing the ability to create interactive graphical applications in the browser. The technology appears to be taking off and there are numerous sources of information as well as excellent development libraries. Check out the WebGL menu option on the left.

Trajectories

Current project partners are making use of trajectories in various forms and I've been developing a tool to help visualise some of the concepts in conjunction with Chris Greenhalgh. The tool has evolved and we are currently assessing the potential of expanding it to other sectors. Check out the TrjectASketch link on the left.
